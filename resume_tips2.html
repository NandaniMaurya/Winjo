How to Write an ATS-Friendly Resume

Posted on May 23, 2024 by John Smith

Introduction

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are used by many companies to screen resumes before they reach a human recruiter. Here's how to write an ATS-friendly resume that increases your chances of getting noticed.

1. Use a Simple Layout

Stick to a clean and simple layout. Avoid using fancy formatting, graphics, or tables, as they can confuse the ATS and cause your resume to be rejected.

2. Include Relevant Keywords

Carefully read the job description and include relevant keywords throughout your resume. These keywords should match the skills and qualifications the employer is looking for.

3. Use Standard Headings

Use standard resume headings like "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", and "Contact Information". ATS software is programmed to look for these common sections.

4. Avoid Headers and Footers

Do not place important information in headers or footers, as ATS software may not read them. Keep all essential details in the main body of the document.

5. Save as a Word Document or Plain Text

While PDFs are widely accepted, some ATS software may have trouble reading them. To be safe, submit your resume as a Word document (.docx) or plain text file (.txt).

6. Focus on Relevant Experience

Highlight your most relevant work experience and skills. Tailor your resume for each job application to show that you are a strong fit for the specific position.

7. Use Bullet Points

Use bullet points to organize information clearly and make it easier for the ATS and recruiters to read. Bullet points help to break up large blocks of text.

Conclusion

By following these tips, you can create a resume that not only passes through the ATS but also stands out to human recruiters. Remember, the goal is to make your resume as clear and relevant as possible.
